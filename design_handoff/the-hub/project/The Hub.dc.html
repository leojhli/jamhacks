Chemical Equilibrium

Core idea

At equilibrium, the forward and reverse reaction rates are equal — concentrations stop changing, but both reactions continue. The position of equilibrium is described by the equilibrium constant K.

ICE tables

Track Initial, Change, and Equilibrium concentrations. Changes follow the stoichiometric ratios; solve for the unknown using K.

Le Châtelier's principle

A system at equilibrium shifts to counteract stress — added reactant shifts forward; increased pressure favors fewer gas moles; raising temperature favors the endothermic direction.

Common mistake: forgetting that pure solids and liquids are excluded from the K expression.
EASY
Write the K expression for N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃.
MEDIUM
Given K and initial concentrations, solve an ICE table for equilibrium [products].
HARD
Predict and justify the shift when pressure is doubled on a mixed-mole-count system.

Chemistry · Equilibrium unit. The Hub compares your notes against the teacher's.

Teacher Notes · Ms. Tan
Equilibrium constant K — ratio of product to reactant concentrations at equilibrium, each raised to its coefficient.
ICE tables — set up Initial / Change / Equilibrium rows; changes follow stoichiometry.
Le Châtelier — systems shift to relieve stress (concentration, pressure, temperature).
Reaction quotient Q — compare Q to K to predict direction of shift.
My Notes · Alex
K = products over reactants at equilibrium. Bigger K = more products.
Le Châtelier — push the system, it pushes back. Add heat → shifts toward endothermic side.
Q vs K — if Q < K, shifts right.
